# Social Media Do's and Don'ts



|**The Do's**|**The Don'ts**|
|-|-|
|Update privacy settings regularly. Platforms change their terms often; make sure your "private" posts stay that way.|Don't overshare your location. Avoid tagging your exact dorm or live-posting your current spot; wait until you've left.|
|Connect with faculty and mentors. Use LinkedIn or professional X (Twitter) accounts to build a bridge to your future career.|Don't engage with trolls. Getting into a heated, public argument rarely ends well and stays searchable forever.|
|Highlight your achievements. Post about projects, volunteer work, or clubs. It’s a living resume.|Don't post "vent" sessions about professors. Even if you're frustrated, keep academic complaints off the record.|
|Audit your "tagged" photos. Untag yourself from anything that doesn't align with the image you want an employer to see.|Don't post illegal or policy-breaking acts. This includes underage drinking or anything that violates your student code of conduct.|
|Take intentional breaks. Use "Do Not Disturb" modes during finals or lectures to stay focused on the real world.|Don't mistake "likes" for self-worth. The algorithm is designed to keep you scrolling, not to validate your value as a person.|
