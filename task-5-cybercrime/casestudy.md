# Case Study: The "Urgent Payroll" Phishing Attack



#### **What is Phishing?**

Phishing is a deceptive practice where cybercriminals masquerade as a trusted entity—such as a bank, a university department, or a popular service provider—to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information. This typically includes login credentials, credit card numbers, or Social Security data.



#### **The Anatomy of an Attack (Step-by-Step)**

In this realistic scenario, a university administrative assistant is targeted through a coordinated email campaign:

1. **The Hook:** The victim receives an email that appears to be from the University’s HR Director. The subject line reads: **"URGENT: Discrepancy in your 2026 Tax Withholding Statement."**
2. **The Pressure:** The message uses high-pressure tactics, claiming that if the recipient does not "verify" their details within two hours, their next paycheck will be frozen.
3. **The Trap:** The assistant clicks a button labeled "View Statement," which redirects them to a "spoof" login page. This page is a near-perfect visual replica of the university’s actual internal portal.
4. **The Theft:** Once the assistant enters their username and password, the attacker captures these credentials in real-time.



#### **Targets and Consequences**

While anyone can be a target, attackers often focus on **employees with access to financial systems** or **new students** who may be less familiar with official digital procedures.

The consequences are severe. For the individual, it often results in **identity theft** and diverted funds. For the institution, a single compromised account can provide a "beachhead" for a larger **ransomware attack**, potentially leading to the exposure of private records for thousands of students and faculty, resulting in massive legal and reputational damage.
