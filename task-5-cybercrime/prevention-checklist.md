# Stay Safe Online: College Student Checklist



Staying safe online is just as important as keeping your physical belongings secure on campus. Between public Wi-Fi, UPI transactions, and academic portals, students are prime targets for cyber threats. Use this checklist to audit your digital security.



---



### The Essential Checklist



* **Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Don't rely on passwords alone. Use MFA (authenticator apps or SMS) for your university portals, Gmail, and social media accounts to ensure a leaked password isn't enough to lock you out.

* **Secure Your UPI PIN:** Never share your UPI PIN with anyone, including people claiming to be bank officials. Remember: **You do not need to enter your PIN to receive money**, only to send it.

* **Verify QR Codes Before Scanning:** Be cautious of random QR codes in cafes or sent via WhatsApp claiming you’ve won a prize. Scammers use these to trigger "Collect Requests" that drain your bank account.

* **Use a VPN on Campus/Public Wi-Fi:** Public networks in hostels or libraries are often unencrypted. Use a trusted VPN to prevent "Man-in-the-Middle" attacks where hackers intercept your data.

* **Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, and contacts. Delete "loan apps" or "photo editors" that demand unnecessary permissions, as these are often used for blackmail or data theft.

* **Fact-Check Job/Internship Offers:** Be wary of "work from home" offers on Telegram or WhatsApp that ask for a "security deposit" or "processing fee." Real companies will never ask you to pay for a job.

* **Practice Social Media Privacy:** Set your profiles to private and be selective with friend requests. Avoid posting your location, student ID card, or boarding passes in real-time to prevent doxing and identity theft.

* **Keep Software Updated:** Don't ignore those "Update Available" pop-ups on your laptop or phone. These updates often contain critical patches for security vulnerabilities that hackers exploit.

### Reporting Cyber Crime in India



If you or someone you know falls victim to a scam, financial fraud, or online harassment, take action immediately. Time is of the essence.



**Official Reporting Channels:**  
* **National Helpline:** Dial **1930** (Available 24/7)  
* **Web Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)



**Note:** For financial fraud, the first **two hours** (the "Golden Hour") are critical. Reporting to 1930 immediately increases the chances of the bank freezing the stolen funds before the scammer can withdraw them.
